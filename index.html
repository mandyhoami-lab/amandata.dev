Background

I have always aspired to be a clinical scientist. I am both a second-generation Vietnamese American and a current Psychology B.A. student at San Diego State University. My quantitative research interests span clinical psychology and neuroscience, personality (Big 5 / HEXACO) facets and environmental factors, human relationships, education systems, early life development, and language acquisition.

* PTSD & Complex PTSD (CPTSD), neurodevelopmental disorders (female presentation), socialization and human development, borderline personality disorder (BPD), antisocial personality disorder (ASPD), narcissistic personality disorder (NPD), obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), dissociative identity disorder (DID).

I am currently spending the year volunteering in neuroscience research labs at SDSU to build technical skill and a more biological foundation, supplementing my theoretical knowledge of the field. I plan to apply to a mix of US/UK social psychology Ph.D. programs in my first cycle, during my last year of university, following the recent NSF budget cuts.

I have about a year of experience in public health / environmental tobacco research focusing on indoor-environment policy, as well as several months running EEG sessions with healthy adult participants — both at SDSU. In the long term, I aim to stay in academia. I've presented my research at the SDSU Student Symposium and the 3rd Annual Research Symposium on Tobacco and the Environment; the former won one of the top two poster presentations for 2026 in the College of Sciences.

This site's theme is dedicated to two of my favorite things: clinical psychology, and the classic board game Othello.

Tobacco Smoke Remediation Practices in San Diego County (ongoing)

Policy Center for Tobacco and the Environment (CTE), San Diego State University

Mentors: Dr. Georg Matt, PhD, Dr. Lydia Greiner, DrPH — with Samantha Yurek

I prepared a full background-and-methods report for a mixed-methods study investigating residential tobacco smoke remediation and thirdhand smoke contamination, after developing a database of 100 remediation and cleaning businesses and certified professionals using IICRC records, business directories, and public databases. I also contributed to recruitment protocol design and implemented purposive and snowball sampling strategies to reach professionals with tobacco smoke remediation experience.

Smoking and Vaping Policies in Newly Constructed, State-Funded Multiunit Housing in California

Policy Research Center for Tobacco and the Environment, San Diego State University

Mentors: Dr. Georg Matt, PhD, Dr. Lydia Greiner, DrPH — with Gunnar Wooldridge

I integrated administrative and survey data from 281 newly constructed state-subsidized housing developments to evaluate implementation of smoking and vaping policies, and conducted structured qualitative coding of interview and survey data to operationalize lease policies and enforcement procedures into thematic categories. I synthesized survey responses from 57 California affordable housing properties to identify patterns in lease policy inclusion and enforcement, and interpreted findings to surface inconsistencies, ambiguous enforcement mechanisms, and gaps in lease disclosure.

Awarded top poster presentation in the College of Sciences at the 2026 SDSU Student Symposium (S3), and also presented at the Third Annual Research Symposium on Tobacco and the Environment.
